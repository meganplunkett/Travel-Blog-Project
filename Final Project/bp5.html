BREMEN

by Mia and Megan

Overview

Bremen is a city municipality in Northern Germany. It is the second largest city in Northern Germany, after Hamburg. It contains many historical galleries and museums, but also has a reputation as a working-class city. It is also the home to a popular fairy tale "The Town Musicians of Bremen", which was published in Grimms' Fairy Tales in 1819. Another fun fact aboout Bremen is that every year, at the end of October, it hosts the country's oldest festival-fair, called Freimarkt, which attracts millions.

Interesting Things to See

As Bremen is home to the fairy tale, "The Town Musicians of Bremen", they have a statue to commemorate it. The statue sits near the town hall, a very impressive renaissance style building that dates back to the early 15th century. It is a must to see both of these things, and it works out that you are able to see them in the same area! Many of the key destinations are in the Marktplatz, which also has historic gabled houses, which you cannot find everywhere in Germany. Another interesting thing to see is Bottcherstrasse. This is a street in the historic center of Bremen, and it contains very unusual architecture and interesting shops. I would also recommend to go walk around the river, if the weather permits.

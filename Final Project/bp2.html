MÜNSTER

by Mia and Megan

Overview

Perhaps the most known of the German cities, Berlin shines brightly as the capital of Germany. The city hosts the greatest amount of citizens and also Angela Merkel- the Chancellor of Germany herself. As Americans living in this city, our experiences had not only consisted of fascinating tours, centering around the Nazi Regime and the Berlin Wall, but a ton of cultural opportunities and excursions. Berlin is seen as Germany's own cultural melting pot, where refugees, foreigners, and natives alike can enjoy what the city has to offer. This may include a thrift store hunt in the neighborhood of Kreuzberg or even a short train ride away to Charlottenburg to visit the Charlottenburg Palace. Read on to hear about some of our not-so-typical recommendations for the Berlin tourist.

Chistmas Markets

Berlin has not one or two, but rather several Christmas Markets that will keep one entertained for weeks. My personal favorite is the Gerdarmenmarkt Christmas Market, which should honestly be more referred to as a small village than as a market. You pay a fee of 1 euro to get in, but have the privilege of visiting hundreds of stands of crafts, cheeses, and Glühwein (mulled wine). However, I usually order a hot chocolate with Baileys and whipped cream on top. So the Gendarmenmarket features crafts of no other, and also has hourly shows with acrobats, comedians, and audience interaction (stay towards the back if you don't want to be called on and rained on with surprise glitter). One of my favorites things was also the giant lit Christmas tree, which stood high over the market. I would recommend market for anyone. -Megan

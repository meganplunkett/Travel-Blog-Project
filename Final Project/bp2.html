MÜNSTER

by Mia and Megan

Overview

Münster, Germany sits in the province of "Nordrhein Westfalen", which is also home to cities like Düsseldorf and Cologne. A city of over 300,000 citizens, a large portion of the population are students of the University of Münster, which makes Münster both a college town and wonderful place to raise a family. You can find the bustling city of brown brick roads, old architecture, and bikes more appealing, or perhaps the sprawling fields between neighborhoods with animals and intermittent red brick houses. You can go to the frequented bars, or find a museum, which tells of the Münster Promenade- a kingdom wall-turned-bike path. Either way, this city has something to offer any person.

"Jugendaustausch" Fresno-Münster 2019

I have an interesting take on Münster, because I first saw it as a guest and student and then later more as a civilian.

In 2017, I took part in an exchange program, because my city (Fresno, California) and Münster are "Partner Cities". When I first saw Münster, I was shocked by its architecture and by its mere colors. The grass was so bright, and the roofs so red, and I was having the time of my life with my exchange partner Kathi, who is still a best friend to this day. I also met my current boyfriend, Joris, who took part in the program. Since 2017, I have been to Münster more than 10 times and consider it another home for me.

-Megan (fourth from the right)
